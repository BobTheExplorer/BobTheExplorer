The Pratt Estate was condemned then purchased by Nassau County in first few months of 1975. The acquisition of the land had long been anticipated, considering that various plans and proposals had been plotted years before the 3-4 million-dollar purchase. One of these plans included turning the premises into a "sanitary" landfill to quell the overflow of trash within the city of Glen Cove. Most residents opposed the improper use of the land, with about 200 of them appeared before the City Council on February 8th, 1972. Another included the construction of 294 condominiums that occupied most of the space on the 212-acre property. Talks between the developer of this plan, Barton A. Forbes, and the then mayor at the time, Vincent A. Suozzi, bore no fruit.
